# 12. Architectural Boundaries and Non-prescription

**Part:** 2 — Architecture of Human-AI Co-evolution  
**Status:** Draft Rev001  
**Type:** Informative / Reference Architecture

IdeaMark Core intentionally defines architectural roles and relationships rather than the complete contents of future intellectual activities.

This boundary is not a limitation of the Core.

It is a design requirement for long-term applicability.

## 12.1 Purpose

The purpose of this section is to clarify what Part 2 does not prescribe.

IdeaMark Core aims to remain usable across long historical time horizons, future media, future AI systems, future social practices, future knowledge representations, and future forms of human-AI collaboration.

To achieve that, Core must avoid over-defining concepts whose contents depend on Situation, Projection, domain practice, social judgment, or future intellectual activity.

## 12.2 Core Defines Roles and Connections

IdeaMark Core defines roles such as:

- Original Source;
- Projection;
- IdeaMark document;
- Observation;
- Situation Vector;
- Human-AI Intellectual Activity;
- Feedback;
- Capability-oriented co-evolution.

It also defines how these roles may be connected in a reconstruction cycle.

It does not define all possible contents of those roles.

This means the Core describes an architecture for future intellectual activity without closing the future of that activity.

## 12.3 Authority Is Contextual to Generation and Reconstruction

IdeaMark Core does not declare that a particular Original Source is universally authoritative.

An Original Source is treated as authority within the context of generating or using an IdeaMark document under a Projection.

```text
Original Source
        x
Projection
        ↓
IdeaMark Document
```

In this relationship, the Original Source is the material basis from which the Projection-guided IdeaMark document is constructed.

That does not make the source universally true, complete, unbiased, lawful, ethical, or final.

It only means that, for that construction and reconstruction activity, the source is the material to which the IdeaMark document should remain traceable.

## 12.4 Observation Is Situation-dependent

Core does not define a fixed boundary for Observation.

What should count as relevant Observation depends on Situation, Projection, cost, urgency, risk, purpose, and available capability.

For example, a person's clothing color in a disaster photograph may be irrelevant to general evacuation guidance.

The same detail may become highly relevant if that person later needs to be identified or located.

Thus Observation cannot be permanently defined by a universal list of properties.

Core recognizes that observations may be used to construct Situation representations, but it does not prescribe what must be observed in every case.

## 12.5 Intellectual Activity Is Not Enumerated

Core does not enumerate all possible forms of Human-AI Intellectual Activity.

It does not define a closed taxonomy of reasoning, learning, designing, deciding, explaining, discovering, creating, negotiating, governing, or acting.

The contents of intellectual activity emerge through Situation, Projection, Original Source, participants, domain practice, and future context.

If Core attempted to enumerate intellectual activity, it would quickly become obsolete.

Part 2 therefore treats Human-AI Intellectual Activity as an architectural role in the reconstruction cycle, not as a complete data model.

## 12.6 Feedback Is Recognized but Not Fixed

Feedback is part of the architecture because intellectual activity changes future situations.

However, Core does not define a universal feedback data structure.

A result of intellectual activity may become:

- a new Observation;
- a new Original Source;
- a revised Projection;
- a regenerated IdeaMark document;
- a human practice;
- an organizational memory;
- an AI training or evaluation trace;
- a social or institutional record;
- nothing persistent, if no record is preserved.

Core recognizes this possibility without requiring that all such outcomes be stored, measured, categorized, or standardized.

The architectural point is that intellectual activity may leave traces that later participate in new reconstruction cycles.

## 12.7 Capability Is a Long-horizon Orientation

Capability expansion is an orientation of the ecosystem, not a required measurement for every IdeaMark use.

Core does not require implementations to calculate how much capability increased after each reconstruction activity.

Capability may be understood broadly as the expanding possibility of meaningful intellectual activity among humans, AI systems, organizations, and future societies.

This expansion may only be visible over long time horizons.

A century of accumulated records, Projections, IdeaMark documents, and practices may reveal that the ecosystem became more capable, even if no single event produced a measurable capability score.

Capability expansion therefore guides design direction without becoming a mandatory metric.

## 12.8 Record Creation and Future Potential

If an activity leaves no trace, later intellectual activity may be unable to learn from it, question it, reinterpret it, or build on it.

For this reason, recording can be valuable even when the immediate significance of a record is unclear.

A casual photograph, a minor experiment log, a sensor trace, a draft, a conversation, or a discarded design note may later become important under a future Situation and Projection.

Core does not require that everything be recorded.

Cost, privacy, law, ethics, storage, and human judgment matter.

However, Core assumes that preserved traces may become future Original Sources and that their future significance cannot always be known at the time of creation.

## 12.9 Non-prescription Is What Enables Function

Many Core concepts function precisely because their contents are not fixed.

Original Source remains useful because future source media and contexts remain open.

Observation remains useful because future situations can decide what matters.

Projection remains useful because future reuse strategies can emerge.

Human-AI Intellectual Activity remains useful because future forms of reasoning, collaboration, and creation are not closed.

Feedback remains useful because future traces may take many forms.

Capability remains useful because it orients the ecosystem beyond short-term output optimization.

Non-prescription is therefore not absence of design.

It is the design choice that gives IdeaMark Core a long time horizon.

## 12.10 Non-goals

Part 2 does not define:

- a universal theory of truth;
- a universal theory of source authority;
- a fixed list of valid source media;
- a fixed boundary of Observation;
- a required Situation Vector representation;
- a universal Projection taxonomy;
- a closed taxonomy of intellectual activities;
- a universal feedback schema;
- a mandatory capability metric;
- a universal normative priority among individual preference, expert judgment, organizational policy, public safety, law, or social value;
- a requirement to record all possible traces regardless of cost, privacy, law, or ethics.

These may be defined by domains, institutions, implementations, companion specifications, Projections, or future societies.

## 12.11 Design Rationale

IdeaMark Core is intended to be durable across changes in media, AI, social practice, and intellectual civilization.

If Core over-prescribed the contents of Observation, Intellectual Activity, Feedback, or Capability, it would reduce its ability to support future forms of work.

If Core declared universal authority or universal normative priorities, it would embed present assumptions into a system intended for future reinterpretation.

By defining roles and connections while leaving contents open, Core supports reconstruction without freezing meaning.

## 12.12 Summary

IdeaMark Core defines the architecture of connection, not the totality of future intellectual life.

It specifies how Original Sources, Projections, IdeaMark documents, Situation representations, Human-AI Intellectual Activity, and future traces may relate.

It intentionally avoids prescribing contents that must remain open to Situation, Projection, domain practice, and future society.

This is how IdeaMark Core seeks a long historical time horizon.
