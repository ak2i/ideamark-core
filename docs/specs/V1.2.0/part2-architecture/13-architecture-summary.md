# 13. Architecture Summary

**Part:** 2 — Architecture of Human-AI Co-evolution  
**Status:** Draft Rev001  
**Type:** Informative / Reference Architecture

Part 2 defines IdeaMark as an architecture for connecting future intellectual activity to Original Sources through Projection and reusable access structures.

It does not define a final knowledge representation.

It defines how reconstruction may remain possible across changing Situations, participants, media, and future forms of human-AI collaboration.

## 13.1 Central Cycle

The central cycle of Part 2 is:

```text
Observation / Expression / Signal
        ↓
Situation Interpretation
        ↓
Situation Vector
        ↓
Projection Selection / Generation / Adaptation
        ↓
IdeaMark Retrieval / Generation
        ↓
Original Source Access
        ↓
Human-AI Intellectual Activity
        ↓
Action / Explanation / Decision / Trace
        ↓
Situation(t + 1)
```

This cycle is explanatory.

It does not prescribe a required user interface, system architecture, storage design, model architecture, or governance model.

## 13.2 What Part 2 Defines

Part 2 defines architectural roles and relationships:

- Original Source as material treated as authority for a Projection-guided reconstruction activity;
- Projection as a reusable strategy for making sources accessible to future intellectual activity;
- IdeaMark document as a reusable access structure, not final meaning;
- Situation interpretation as the transformation of observations and expressions into Situation representations;
- Human-AI Intellectual Activity as the collaborative role in which reconstruction becomes understanding, judgment, explanation, action, or trace;
- Feedback as the possibility that results and traces may become future Observations, Original Sources, Projections, or practices;
- Capability orientation as the long-horizon direction toward expanded future intellectual possibility.

## 13.3 What Part 2 Does Not Define

Part 2 intentionally does not define:

- a universal truth model;
- a universal source authority model;
- a fixed source media taxonomy;
- a required Projection creation method;
- a universal Projection taxonomy;
- a fixed Observation boundary;
- a required Situation Vector representation;
- a closed taxonomy of intellectual activities;
- a universal feedback schema;
- a mandatory capability metric;
- a required implementation architecture.

This non-prescription is part of the design.

It allows IdeaMark Core to remain applicable to future domains, future AI systems, future media, and future knowledge forms.

## 13.4 Why Projection Matters

Projection matters because interpretation is situated and costly.

A source does not produce a useful interpretation by itself.

A subject, Situation, Projection, and reconstruction process are needed.

Projection provides a reusable direction for reconstruction.

It may be carefully designed, gradually cultivated, experimentally discovered, accidentally successful, private, shared, or standardized.

Core does not prescribe how Projection must be created.

It only requires that Projection can function as a reusable strategy for IdeaMark generation and reconstruction.

## 13.5 Why Original Source Matters

Original Source matters because reconstruction needs material to return to.

IdeaMark does not replace Original Sources with final summaries.

It helps humans and AI systems return to sources under current Situations and Projections.

A source is not limited by medium, caption, current use, or current interpretation.

Its future significance may not be known at the time of creation.

This is why preserving traces can matter, even when immediate value is unclear.

## 13.6 Why Interpretation Cost Matters

The long-term value of IdeaMark does not come from preserving a single interpretation.

It comes from reducing the cost of reconstructing useful interpretations across generations, domains, organizations, and future forms of human-AI intellectual activity.

Interpretation cost includes the cost of creating, understanding, reviewing, maintaining, teaching, and reusing intellectual work.

A powerful method that is too costly to interpret may fail to spread or survive.

A reusable access structure that lowers interpretation cost can make knowledge more usable without fixing meaning.

## 13.7 Why AI Matters

IdeaMark becomes especially practical in AI-enabled environments.

LLMs and multimodal AI systems can help transform partial observations into Situation representations, adapt Projections, retrieve IdeaMark documents, revisit Original Sources, and participate in reconstruction.

However, AI participation does not make AI infallible.

It also does not imply legal personhood or human-equivalent moral status.

AI participates architecturally as part of Human-AI Intellectual Activity.

## 13.8 Why Capability Matters

Capability orientation gives the architecture a long time horizon.

IdeaMark should not optimize only immediate answers.

It should keep future reconstruction possible.

Over time, preserved sources, reusable Projections, lower interpretation cost, and traceable Human-AI Intellectual Activity may allow individuals, organizations, communities, and societies to become more capable.

This expansion need not be measured after every use.

It is a direction for architecture, not a universal metric.

## 13.9 Final Summary

IdeaMark Core Part 2 describes a reference architecture for human-AI co-evolution.

It treats expressions and records as static traces, while recognizing that intellectual activity is dynamic, situated, and future-facing.

It uses Projection to connect Original Sources to future reconstruction without freezing meaning.

It uses IdeaMark documents as reusable access structures rather than final interpretations.

It keeps Observation, Situation, Intellectual Activity, Feedback, and Capability open so that future forms of media, AI, society, and knowledge can participate.

The result is an architecture for lowering interpretation cost and preserving the possibility of future intellectual activity.
