# IdeaMark Core v1.2.0

# Part 1 --- Philosophy

**Status:** Draft for Review

## Foreword

IdeaMark is not a document format, a knowledge base, or an ontology.

IdeaMark is an architectural foundation for making intellectual
activities reusable across people, organizations, domains, and
generations.

Its purpose is not to preserve meaning, but to preserve accessibility to
meaning. Meaning remains in authoritative original sources and is
reconstructed by humans and AI under new situations.

## 1. What IdeaMark Is

IdeaMark is a structural representation of reusable traces that enable
intellectual activities.

It intentionally avoids fixing interpretations, conclusions, or
meanings.

It preserves reusable structures that allow future intellectual
activities to begin from authoritative original sources.

### Design Rationale

Knowledge systems traditionally preserved answers. IdeaMark preserves
the ability to rediscover answers under future conditions.

## 2. Historical Background

Information technologies evolved through complementary stages:

-   Knowledge-based systems preserved explicit knowledge.
-   Information retrieval enabled document discovery.
-   Semantic retrieval improved discovery using similarity.
-   Large language models enabled dynamic interpretation.

IdeaMark complements these layers by indexing reusable structures that
connect situations to authoritative original sources.

## 3. Motivation

Traditional knowledge management required shared interpretations because
coordinated human action depended upon them.

AI-enabled environments fundamentally change this assumption.

Interpretations no longer need to be fixed in advance. They can be
reconstructed dynamically for each individual, organization, situation,
and purpose.

IdeaMark supports this transition.

## 4. AI-Enabled Knowledge Work

AI is treated as a collaboration catalyst rather than a replacement for
human reasoning.

Different participants may receive different interpretations while
remaining connected to the same original sources.

Collaboration therefore depends on shared access to authoritative
sources rather than identical interpretations.

## 5. Core Principles

1.  Separate structure from meaning.
2.  Original sources remain authoritative.
3.  Projection defines knowledge accessibility rather than truth.
4.  Preserve reusable structures instead of fixed interpretations.
5.  Human and AI are equal interpreters.
6.  IdeaMark documents are operational snapshots that may evolve.

## 6. Design Scope

IdeaMark intentionally does not define meaning, universal ontologies,
domain vocabularies, cognitive models, or future limits of intellectual
activity.

## 7. Future Direction

Projection, coordinate models, retrieval architectures, and authoring
methods evolve independently from the Core while remaining
interoperable.

## Closing Statement

IdeaMark is an intellectual activity infrastructure.

It does not preserve how people think.

It preserves reusable structures that enable future humans and AI to
begin thinking from authoritative original sources under new situations.

By reducing the cost of accessing intellectual activities instead of
prescribing interpretations, IdeaMark aims to promote richer
collaboration across disciplines, organizations, cultures, and
generations.
