# IdeaMark Core Philosophy v1.1.1

## 1. Core Philosophy

### 1.1 Background

Human language is not strictly governed by formal grammar.

Instead, it operates within a loosely bounded expressive space:

- Expression > Grammar
- Meaning emerges dynamically
- Communication remains stable despite freedom

This implies:

> There exists a constrained but flexible meaning space in human language.

---

### 1.2 Observation from LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate that:

- Finite structures can generate seemingly infinite expressions
- Generated language remains human-like
- Outputs rarely diverge into meaningless randomness

This suggests:

> LLMs approximate the distribution of acceptable human meaning.

---

### 1.3 Core Hypothesis

IdeaMark is based on the following hypothesis:

> Meaning is not stored in text itself, but emerges through structured interpretation.

---

### 1.4 Design Shift

Previous versions (v1.0.x):

- Meaning = anchorage + role

v1.1.x:

> Meaning = controlled emergence within a structured space

---

### 1.5 Controlled Divergence

IdeaMark introduces the concept of:

> Controlled Divergence Space

Meaning may vary, but only within reusable structural boundaries.

---

### 1.6 Responsibility Separation

| Layer | Responsibility |
|---------|---------|
| LLM | Generate meaning distributions |
| Perspective | Direct interpretation |
| IdeaMark | Constrain structure |

---

### 1.7 Philosophy Summary

1. Meaning must not be fully defined.
2. Meaning must be directionally guided.
3. Structure must constrain interpretation.
4. Divergence must be allowed but controlled.

---

### 1.8 Formal Model

Reality → Projection → Log → IdeaMark Structure → Projection → New Meaning

---

### 1.9 Key Statement

> IdeaMark does not define meaning.
> It defines the conditions under which meaning can emerge without collapsing into chaos.

---

### 1.10 Representation Independence

Meaning and representation are distinct concerns.

Therefore:

> Representation is not Meaning.

IdeaMark standardizes reusable structure, not representation formats.

---

### 1.11 Meaning Is Not Stored

Meaning is not a property permanently embedded within text.

Meaning emerges when:

- a representation is interpreted
- under a particular perspective
- for a particular purpose
- by a particular interpreter

Therefore:

> Meaning is not preserved.
> Only reusable traces of meaning-making are preserved.

Different readers MAY derive different meanings from the same representation.

This is not a defect.

It is a fundamental property of interpretation.

---

### 1.12 Log-Centric Knowledge Reuse

IdeaMark treats documents as records of intellectual activity.

Examples include:

- conversations
- reports
- essays
- observations
- plans
- analyses

These artifacts are not knowledge itself.

They are logs of prior meaning-making processes.

IdeaMark therefore preserves reusable structural traces that allow future interpreters to:

- retrieve
- reinterpret
- recombine
- project

knowledge into new contexts.

Thus:

> IdeaMark is not a storage format for knowledge.
> It is a structure for reusing intellectual activity logs.

---

### 1.13 Payload-Agnostic Core

IdeaMark Core is payload-agnostic.

Payload formats may evolve independently from the IdeaMark model.

IdeaMark is responsible for:

- Entity boundaries
- Occurrence activation
- Section interpretation
- Perspective guidance
- Relation structure

Payload formats remain external concerns.

Therefore:

> IdeaMark is a meaning-structure framework, not a knowledge-representation format.

---

### 1.14 Perspective and Projection

Perspective is not meaning itself.

Nor is it the complete projection process.

Perspective records a reusable clue regarding the interpretive direction under which a log was created or analyzed.

Projection is the active process through which meaning is generated.

Thus:

- Perspective is a trace of projection
- Projection is a generator of meaning

Future readers MAY use Perspective as guidance when performing new projections.

However:

> Perspective does not guarantee identical meaning outcomes.

Meaning remains an emergent result of interpretation.

---

### 1.15 Knowledge Reuse Principle

Knowledge reuse is achieved by separating:

- Representation
- Meaning Boundary
- Contextual Activation
- Interpretation Direction

This separation allows:

- heterogeneous knowledge formats
- cross-domain reuse
- perspective-dependent retrieval
- compositional synthesis

without requiring a single universal representation format.

---

### 1.16 Core Positioning

IdeaMark Core should be understood as:

> A payload-agnostic meaning-structure framework for organizing,
> retrieving, reprojecting, and recomposing reusable traces of
> intellectual activity.

IdeaMark does not preserve meaning.

It preserves structures that help future interpreters generate meaning again.
