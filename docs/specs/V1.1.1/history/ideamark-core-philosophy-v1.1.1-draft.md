# IdeaMark Core Philosophy v1.1.1

## 1. Core Philosophy

### 1.1 Background

Human language is not strictly governed by formal grammar.

Instead, it operates within a loosely bounded expressive space:

- Expression > Grammar
- Meaning emerges dynamically
- Communication remains stable despite freedom

This implies:

> There exists a constrained but flexible meaning space in human language.

---

### 1.2 Observation from LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate that:

- Finite structures can generate seemingly infinite expressions
- Generated language remains human-like
- Outputs rarely diverge into meaningless randomness

This suggests:

> LLMs approximate the distribution of acceptable human meaning.

---

### 1.3 Core Hypothesis

IdeaMark is based on the following hypothesis:

> Meaning is not stored in text itself, but emerges through structured interpretation.

---

### 1.4 Design Shift

Previous versions (v1.0.x):

- Meaning = anchorage + role

v1.1.x:

> Meaning = controlled emergence within a structured space

---

### 1.5 Controlled Divergence

IdeaMark introduces the concept of:

> Controlled Divergence Space

Meaning may vary, but only within reusable structural boundaries.

---

### 1.6 Responsibility Separation

| Layer | Responsibility |
|---------|---------|
| LLM | Generate meaning distributions |
| Perspective | Direct interpretation |
| IdeaMark | Constrain structure |

---

### 1.7 Philosophy Summary

1. Meaning must not be fully defined.
2. Meaning must be directionally guided.
3. Structure must constrain interpretation.
4. Divergence must be allowed but controlled.

---

### 1.8 Formal Model

Text → Entity Candidates → Perspective → Meaning Projection → IdeaMark Structure

---

### 1.9 Key Statement

> IdeaMark does not define meaning.
> It defines the conditions under which meaning can emerge without collapsing into chaos.

---

### 1.10 Representation Independence

Meaning and representation are distinct concerns.

The same meaning may be represented through:

- plain text
- markdown
- OKF
- TPCG
- JSON
- YAML
- future formats

Likewise, the same representation may support multiple meanings under different perspectives.

Therefore:

> Representation is not Meaning.

IdeaMark does not standardize representation itself.

Instead, it standardizes the structural conditions under which representations become reusable meaning units.

---

### 1.11 Payload-Agnostic Core

IdeaMark Core is payload-agnostic.

Payload formats may evolve independently from the IdeaMark model.

IdeaMark is responsible for:

- Entity boundaries
- Occurrence activation
- Section interpretation
- Perspective guidance
- Relation structure

Payload formats remain external concerns.

Therefore:

> IdeaMark is a meaning-structure framework, not a knowledge-representation format.

---

### 1.12 Knowledge Reuse Principle

Knowledge reuse is achieved by separating:

- Representation
- Meaning Boundary
- Contextual Activation
- Interpretation Direction

This separation allows:

- heterogeneous knowledge formats
- cross-domain reuse
- perspective-dependent retrieval
- compositional synthesis

without requiring a single universal representation format.

---

### 1.13 Core Positioning

IdeaMark Core should be understood as:

> A payload-agnostic meaning-structure framework for organizing, interpreting, retrieving, and recomposing heterogeneous knowledge representations.
